def recovery_calculation (row):
    c = row['rougher.output.concentrate_au']
    f = row['rougher.input.feed_au']
    t = row['rougher.output.tail_au']
    up  = c*(f-t)
    down = f*(c-t)
    
    if up <=0.0:
        up = 0.0
    
    if down<=0.0:
        down = 0.0001
    
    total = (up/down)*100
    
    if total>100.0:
        total = 100.0
        
    return total

3. Building the model¶

3.1. A function to calculate the final sMAPE value¶

To solve the problem, we will need a new metric. It is called sMAPE, symmetric Mean Absolute Percentage Error. It is similar to MAE, but is expressed in relative values instead of absolute ones. Why is it symmetrical? It equally takes into account the scale of both the target and the prediction.

In [31]:
def smape (target,prediction):
    up = abs(target-prediction)
    down = (abs(target)+abs(prediction))/2
    fraction = up/down
    sigma = fraction.sum()
    total = (sigma/len(target))*100
    return total
In [32]:
def final_smape (ro_t,ro_p,fi_t,fi_p):
    ro = 0.25*smape(ro_t,ro_p)
    fi = 0.75*smape(fi_t,fi_p)
    total = ro + fi
    return total

Technological Process¶

Mined ore undergoes primary processing to get the ore mixture or rougher feed, which is the raw material for flotation (also known as the rougher process). After flotation, the material is sent to two-stage purification.

1. Flotation¶

Gold ore mixture is fed into the float banks to obtain rougher Au concentrate and rougher tails (product residues with a low concentration of valuable metals). The stability of this process is affected by the volatile and non-optimal physicochemical state of the flotation pulp (a mixture of solid particles and liquid).

2. Purification¶

The rougher concentrate undergoes two stages of purification. After purification, we have the final concentrate and new tails.
